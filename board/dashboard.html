Bit-DQN Experiment Dashboard

sequence-length scaling of binary policies Author: Sujie Hu @ Tsinghua University (issjaaa312@gmail.com)
Metric definitions success exact match bit-acc per-bit Hamming prefix-acc leading-prefix ratio click to expand

Each variant is evaluated on random binary targets at every sequence length n ∈ [1, 50]. Three complementary metrics are reported:

  • success rate. Fraction of evaluation episodes whose generated sequence matches the target exactly (Hamming distance = 0). Strictest, episode-level metric.
  • bit accuracy. Mean per-position bit agreement, averaged over episodes: 1 − Hamming(gen, tgt) / n. Random guessing ≈ 0.5; perfect policy = 1.0.
  • prefix accuracy. For each episode, the longest correct leading prefix divided by n, then averaged. Measures how far the policy gets before its first mistake.

All DQN variants share the same training budget — episodes = 1200, hidden_dim = 128, eval_episodes = 200; baselines do not train (episodes = 0, eval_episodes = 1000).
